The idea

Typically when someone comes to me, they have a pretty good idea what it is they want.

Some software developers just take these ideas and run with them - producing exactly what's in the client's head.

I prefer to innovate together with the client, finding the best union of the client's domain expertise and my understanding of the technology.

A good example is mobile apps - is that really what you need? Or is it an unnecessary expense, when solutions like PWA exist?

The right questions

The most important part of innovation is asking the right questions.

Questions to help understanding the domain better and respecting the client's knowledge

Questioning whether something is technologically possible

Questioning the approach. Is it financially feasible - is there a simpler solution that can provide the same results?

Taking into account trends or changes in the industry - things I have to keep on top of but the client might not know. For example, PWA, SPA, changes in autoplay functionality, etc.

Careview example: does it really need to be a mobile app, with all the bureaucracy/complication? Or is PWA the solution? iOS 11.3

Thinking like a machine

So much of software engineering is breaking free of human thought and thinking like a computer.

Examples:

  • Relational data / REST - breaking your world up into resources and verbs.
  • Security - how do computers develop trust?
  • Separation of concerns - content, presentation and user interaction are. different things.
  • Testing & regression - machines behaving like people. What people? Design for the most unusual cases.

Careview example: how to secure the data so it can't be used to target vulnerable people. Encryption in transit vs at rest.

Iterative development

Innovation is an ongoing process.

The trick is to find the smallest step possible and get that in front of users as quickly as possible, get feedback, and let that inform the next direction.

You can do this over and over forever - sometimes what you have made after a few is nowhere near what you thought you were going to make, but it's miles better for it.

Regression testing stops later iterations becoming a nightmare to maintain.

Careview example: prototype developed swiftly without tests. Discarded. New one GPS-only at first. Usability workshop. V2.
